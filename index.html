The Birth of Everything

Journey through 13.8 billion years of cosmic evolution

13.8 billion years ago

The Big Bang

The universe begins as a singularity and rapidly expands in a fraction of a second, creating space, time, and all fundamental forces.

380,000 years after Big Bang

First Light

The universe cools enough for electrons and protons to combine, making it transparent to light. The cosmic microwave background radiation is released.

400 million years after Big Bang

First Stars Ignite

Gravity pulls together the first clumps of matter, igniting the first generation of massive stars that forge heavy elements in their cores.

1 billion years after Big Bang

Galaxy Formation

The first galaxies form as stars cluster together under gravity, creating the cosmic web structure we see today.

4.6 billion years ago

Solar System Birth

Our solar system forms from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust. The Sun ignites, and planets begin to accrete from the surrounding disk.

3.8 billion years ago

Life Emerges

The first simple life forms appear on Earth in the primordial oceans, beginning the long journey of biological evolution.

Today

The Present

After 13.8 billion years of cosmic evolution, we exist to contemplate our place in this vast, expanding universe.
